Trained as a garage door technician before starting Iron Wolf, with a background repairing and installing residential and light-commercial doors across the Chicago metro. Started Iron Wolf to run the shop the way I always thought it should be run — one job at a time, one honest quote at a time.

What that means for you

You are not being handed off between a dispatcher, a call-taker, and a rotating crew of subcontractors. The person who diagnoses the door is the person who repairs it and the person who stands behind the warranty. If something is not right after the visit, you call the same number and the same person answers.

Why start with a new shop

Because a one-truck owner-operator is the version of the trade with the fewest layers between you and the work. No sales quotas rolling down from a call center, no rotating techs learning the job on your door, no incentive to sell you replacements when a repair will do. Just one person who wants five-star reviews more than the next upsell.

How we earn the first hundred customers

Honest quotes, small first-visit discounts for early customers, and a genuine ask for a Google review afterward if the work was good. We are building the review count in public — no fake testimonials, no purchased reviews. If you leave one, it will be your name and your words.

How visits work

What a homeowner should expect from the appointment.

Diagnosis first

Balance, spring tension, tracks, rollers, hinges, cables, safety sensors, and opener behavior — all checked before we recommend any parts.

Photos before parts

When a part has failed, we show it to you and explain what happened before the repair is approved.

Written estimate

The number is reviewed before work starts, including labor and the parts being installed. No parts come off the truck until you sign off.

Test cycle together

When the job is done, the door is cycled, the work is reviewed, and the warranty is handed over in writing.
