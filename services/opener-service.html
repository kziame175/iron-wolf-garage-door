Common symptoms

What we see on opener service calls.

One click, no movement

Usually a dead capacitor or logic board. Sometimes a snapped drive gear on older chain openers.

Closes an inch then reverses

Photo eyes out of alignment or lens covered in dust — the opener assumes an obstruction.

Remote works, wall button doesn't (or vice versa)

Different power paths. The wire, button, or logic board port is usually the culprit.

Motor runs, door does not move

Broken trolley release, snapped drive belt, or stripped worm gear on chain drives.

Keypad not accepting code

Battery, code reset needed, or receiver-side pairing lost after a power surge.

Wi-Fi/MyQ dropped

Router band changes, firmware, or a failing radio module — often fixable without a new opener.

FAQ

Opener Service questions we hear.

Belt-drive vs. chain-drive — which is better?

Belt-drives are quieter and lower maintenance, chain-drives are slightly cheaper and more tolerant of extreme cold. For a bedroom above the garage, choose belt.

Do I need a Wi-Fi opener?

Only if you want phone control, delivery access, or vacation monitoring. LiftMaster's MyQ platform is the industry standard.

How long do openers last?

10–15 years typical; belt-drives with steady maintenance often stretch to 18–20.

Can you convert an old two-piece rail to a modern opener?

Yes. Full removal of the old rail, header bracket, and trolley; new opener installed with proper reinforcement.
