Common symptoms

What we see on tune-ups calls.

Door has gotten noisier

Most noise comes from dry hinges, worn steel rollers, and loose lag bolts — all fixed in one visit.

Feels heavier than last year

Spring tension drifts as coils fatigue. We measure and re-tension into spec.

You just moved in

New homeowners rarely know the door's history. A tune-up sets a baseline you can trust.

Winter cold snap coming

Cold shrinks steel and stiffens grease. Pre-winter tune-up prevents frozen bottom seals and slow opens.

Sensor light blinking sometimes

Cabling, alignment, and connector fatigue — small fixes that avoid mid-cycle reverses.

Old rollers rattling

Nylon-sealed roller upgrade is one of the highest-value tune-up add-ons on a busy door.

How it goes

Our tune-ups process.

01

Balance test with the opener disconnected — should hold at waist height without help.

02

Lubricate hinges, springs, and bearings with garage-door-rated lithium grease (never WD-40).

03

Inspect and photograph cables, drums, and end bearings.

04

Test opener force, travel limits, and safety reverse against a 2×4 on the floor.

05

Check weather seals: bottom astragal, side stops, and top header.

FAQ

Tune-Ups questions we hear.

How often should I tune up my door?

Once a year for typical use. Twice a year if the door cycles 6+ times a day or if you have a shop garage.

What do you lubricate — and what should I never spray?

White lithium or silicone spray on hinges, springs, and bearings. Never WD-40 (it strips grease) and never grease the tracks (rollers should roll, not slide).

Can a tune-up extend spring life?

Modestly. What it really extends is opener life, roller life, and bearing life — all of which cost more to replace than the spring itself.

Do you offer a maintenance plan?

Yes. Annual tune-ups with priority scheduling and a small discount on any repair caught during the visit.
